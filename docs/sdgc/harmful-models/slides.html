When the Model Is the Harm

Spectacle Without Accountability

  • Professional wrestling presents conflict and resolution without the substance of either
  • Audience knows it is performance
  • Performance produces the feeling of stakes without the stakes
  • Regulatory hearings, congressional testimony, and public apologies function the same way
    • Zuckerberg's 2018 Senate testimony produced viral memes and no legislation
    • Facebook's practices unchanged [Barthes1972, Frankfurt2005, Frenkel2021, Mazer1998]

When Rivals Are Financially Interdependent

  • WWF and WCW needed each other
  • Each needed a villain the other could play
  • Apparent rivals in platform markets may have similar interdependencies
  • Financial interdependence between "competitors" explains why competition does not produce expected outcomes
    • Apple and Google are nominally competitors in mobile operating systems
    • Apple receives ~$20B per year from Google to remain the default search engine on iPhones

Distributed Outrage Produces No Change

  • Outrage concentrated on one target can produce sustained pressure
  • Outrage spread across all targets ("everyone is bad") produces commentary, not reform
  • The wrestling model of rotating villains distributes attention so that none is sustained
    • "Techlash" of 2016-2018 produced sustained coverage of every tech company in rotation
    • No major legislation resulted because outrage never concentrated

Regulatory Fines as Performance

  • A fine representing a small fraction of revenue from the illegal activity is not a deterrent
  • It is a licensing fee: pay it and continue
  • Facebook's $5B FTC fine in 2019 was record-setting
  • Also two months of revenue
    • FTC settlement immunized Facebook's executives from personal liability
    • Fine became a pure cost of doing business [Stoller2019]

The MLM Math Problem

  • In a multi-level marketing structure, each participant must recruit additional participants
  • Recruiting tree grows exponentially
  • Market for participants does not
  • Most participants lose money
  • Math guarantees it regardless of effort
    • FTC found in 2011 study that median Herbalife participant made $0 annually while top 1% captured 87% of all distributor income [FitzPatrick2020]

Rare Success Is the Advertisement

  • MLM presentations feature the exceptional cases of success
  • Statistical reality of average outcomes is never the advertisement
  • Successful participants have a financial incentive to recruit, and therefore to advertise success
    • Mary Kay consultants who reach the "Pink Cadillac" level receive a ~$30,000 car and then promote it extensively, making it appear more achievable than the 1-in-1000 odds suggest

Platforms as MLMs

  • Platforms depending on users to generate content and the ties that make leaving costly reproduce the MLM structure at scale
  • Users do the advertising
  • Platform captures the value
  • Participant is simultaneously the product, the salesperson, and the customer
    • YouTube creators generate content that makes YouTube worth visiting and recruit the subscribers that make leaving costly, while YouTube captures the advertising revenue [Srnicek2016]

When the Participant Is Product, Salesperson, and Customer

  • This structure eliminates the need for conventional advertising
  • Concentrates risk and reward asymmetrically: platform profits whether individual participants succeed or not
  • Parallel to MLM is structural, not rhetorical
    • Top 1% of YouTube creators capture 90% of advertising revenue
    • Distribution identical to MLM income structure [Srnicek2016]

Government-Like Functions Acquire Political Protection

  • Yakuza in Japan performed dispute resolution, contract enforcement, and disaster relief
  • Organizations that perform government-like functions become politically difficult to dismantle
  • Governments become dependent on the infrastructure they provide
    • After 2011 Tohoku earthquake, yakuza groups provided supplies and cleanup crews faster than the government
    • Reinforcing their social legitimacy at the moment the government was visibly failing [Adelstein2023, Kaplan2012]

Territorial Control Without Violence

  • Platform market power works like territorial control: exclusive access, penalties for competitors, bundled services
  • Violence not necessary
  • Algorithmic ranking penalties and terms-of-service enforcement suffice
  • Outcome is the same: exclusive territorial control
    • An app downranked in the App Store for using a competing payment system experiences a result structurally identical to a cartel enforcing territorial exclusivity

Symbiotic Accountability Gaps

  • Neither the market nor democratic oversight can easily close gaps created by quasi-governmental private actors
  • Yakuza provided services the state could not or would not provide
  • Removing them left a gap
  • Platforms provide infrastructure that makes dismantling them politically costly
    • Japan's 2011 anti-yakuza legislation reduced gang membership by half but also increased unaffiliated street crime as the regulatory function the yakuza performed disappeared [Adelstein2023]

Political Protection Can Become Vulnerability

  • Political connections providing protection can be converted into vulnerability when political context changes
  • Japanese government's crackdown on the yakuza became possible when the political calculus shifted
  • Platform political protection is contingent on continued usefulness to those in power
    • Twitter/X's Elon Musk aligned the platform with incoming political power in 2024
    • Resulting regulatory protection is contingent on that alignment persisting
