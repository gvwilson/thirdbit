Alternatives and Dead Weight

The Standard Corporation Is One Choice Among Many

  • Public benefit corporations, cooperatives, mutual societies, credit unions, and family firms all exist
  • Each organizes the relationship between risk, reward, and governance differently
  • The investor-owned corporation is one legal form, not the natural form of organization
    • Modern publicly-traded corporation with limited liability and shareholder primacy: a deliberate 19th-century legal invention, not a market discovery [Bakan2005, Kelly2012, Linklater2015]

Public Benefit Corporations and Mutual Societies

  • Public benefit corporations allow directors to consider stakeholder interests alongside shareholder returns
  • Mutual societies owned by members rather than shareholders: insurance mutuals, building societies
  • These structures have operated at large scale for over a century
    • Co-operative Group (UK): supermarkets, funeral homes, insurance
    • Member-owned since 1860s
    • Among country's largest employers

Worker Cooperatives at Scale

  • Workers in a cooperative own the firm and share both control and profit
  • Mondragon (Spain): over 80,000 employees across manufacturing, retail, and finance
  • REI, the outdoor retailer: a consumer cooperative with over 20 million members
    • Mondragon survived the 2008 financial crisis by transferring workers between subsidiaries rather than laying them off [Whyte1991, Morrison1991, Blasi2013, Wainwright2017]

Why the "Can't Scale" Narrative Persists

  • Claim that cooperatives cannot scale is contradicted by the evidence
  • Narrative persists because it serves incumbent investor-owned firms
  • Cooperatives consistently survive economic crises that destroy conventional competitors
    • Also self-fulfilling: cooperative financing harder to access because banks and venture capital are designed for investor-owned models

Mondragon, Desjardins, and the Italian Co-ops

  • Desjardins: Quebec's largest financial institution, and a cooperative
  • Emilia-Romagna (Italy): cooperative sector employs 40% of regional workforce
  • Not small experiments
  • Large-scale, long-running institutions
    • Emilia-Romagna consistently ranks among Europe's wealthiest regions despite no natural resources
    • Cooperative structure credited with distributed prosperity [Ostrom2015, Gordon2001, Thelen1992, Wainwright2017]

Tech's Governance Structure Was a Deliberate Choice

  • Tech's concentrated decision-making and financial upside: a deliberate design, not a default
  • Dual-class share structures entrench founder control regardless of shareholder preferences
  • Structure was chosen
  • Could be chosen differently
    • Alphabet, Meta, and Snap all use dual-class shares: 10 votes per share for founders vs. 1 for ordinary shareholders

Crises Create Openings

  • New Deal labor reforms followed the Depression and mass unemployment
  • UK welfare state followed the Second World War
  • Disasters alone do not produce reforms
  • Organizational capacity determines whether openings are used
    • 1906 San Francisco earthquake: no major labor reforms
    • 1911 Triangle Shirtwaist fire, with a union already organizing, led to factory safety legislation within three years [Beckerman2022, Orleck1995]

Organizational Capacity Determines the Outcome

  • Without pre-existing organizing, crises produce emergency measures that fade
  • With pre-existing organizing, crises can produce durable institutional change
  • Building capacity before the crisis is the work
    • Eight-hour day: not won in response to a crisis
    • Won after decades of organizing that put unions in position to press their advantage [Thompson1963, Roediger1989]

Where Productivity Gains Go

  • Labor productivity has risen consistently since the 1970s
  • Wages have not kept pace with productivity growth in most wealthy countries
  • Distribution of gains is a political question, not a technical one
    • US: productivity grew ~60% between 1979 and 2019
    • Median hourly wages grew ~15%
    • Gap represents political redistribution from wages to capital [Acemoglu2023]

Busyness as Status Signal

  • In many professional cultures, appearing busy signals importance
  • Organizations generate work to justify existing headcount rather than to maximize output
  • Meetings producing minutes no one reads
  • Reports no one acts on
    • Parkinson observed this in the British civil service in 1955: number of officials increased regardless of whether work increased, decreased, or disappeared [Garvin2013, Veblen1899]

Parkinson's Law in Practice

  • Work expands to fill the time available for its completion
  • Cyril Northcote Parkinson observed this in the British civil service in 1955
  • Describes a structural dynamic, not a character flaw
    • Graeber estimated a substantial fraction of professional employment consists of roles whose primary function is maintaining the appearance of productivity [Graeber2018]

A Political Question, Not a Technical One

  • 35-hour workweek in France
  • Four-day week experiments in Iceland and Japan
  • Question is not whether shorter hours are possible but who benefits from the current arrangement
  • Productivity gains captured as profit because people with power prefer it that way
    • Keynes's prediction that technology would enable 15-hour work weeks: correct about the technology, completely wrong about who benefits [Keynes1930, Sandel2020, Cassidy2025]

Building Parallel Institutions

  • Zapatistas of Chiapas, Mexico established self-governing municipalities in 1994 without waiting for the state
  • Built their own health clinics, schools, and justice systems within indigenous communities
  • Operated three decades alongside the formal state, often in opposition to it [Harvey1998]

Redesigning Credit for Excluded Populations

  • Grameen Bank of Bangladesh, founded 1983: lent to rural women with no collateral
  • Replaced collateral with social accountability: small groups of borrowers guaranteed each other's loans
  • Demonstrated that the poor, especially poor women, are creditworthy when lending structures are redesigned around their actual circumstances [Yunus2007]
