A Game of Chess

A solarium. A table with two comfortable chairs, an old man in each. Snow is falling outside. There is a chessboard between the two men; a game is in progress. A clock is ticking. The camera pans in and over the men just as one of them sighs and reaches out to move a knight.

Vienna at night, 1955. A younger version of the man who just moved slips over a wall and enters a noisy bar through the back door.

The solarium; the clock is ticking. The second man sips his coffee, then puts the cup down and moves his rook.

Inside the bar, people are dancing, laughing, and talking in German and English. A younger version of the second man is sitting at a table with a pretty young woman. He turns suddenly and stares directly into the camera.

Solarium. The first man moves his knight again.

The bar, in slow motion. The first man pulls a gun out from under his jacket.

Solarium. The second man looks at the board, scratching his head, while the first man pours a fresh cup of coffee and stirs in some sugar. Finally the second man sighs and moves a pawn to cover his rook.

The bar, still in slow motion. The young woman rises from her chair, mouth open to shout or scream. She pushes the second man aside as the first man fires.
