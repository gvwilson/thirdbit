<p><a href="http://www.theawl.com/2013/02/how-to-save-college">Clay Shirky's latest piece</a>, which points out that offline colleges are broken, has been attracting a fair bit of attention. What <em>isn't</em> is the way he and others are trying to frame the debate, which goes something like, "Yeah, MOOCs aren't perfect, but have you actually looked at what most non-elite colleges actually offer?" This boils down to, "Our bad is slightly less bad than their bad," but blithely ignores the fact that something much better than either exists.
