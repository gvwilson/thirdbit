A bookish girl who lives on a flying mountain finds her home and her life thrown off course, and must come to terms with her people's history, and possibly with the end of the world as she knows it.  With a strong female protagonist, intrigue, swordplay, magic, and a library to die for, "The Bookster's Apprentice" also touches on the deeper question of who places belong to and why.

---

On the flying mountain of Avaunt, a young girl named Erileine becomes apprentice to Razi, a wandering scholar cataloging the library in the Captain's House. When the mountain abruptly changes course for the first time in centuries, Erileine's childhood tormentor Fraederiq falls to his death while chasing her.

Along with others from her village, Erileine travels to the mountain's capital, where she and Razi meet the captain, a smug magician named Thomas, and a young thief named Piaerre. There, they discover that the mountain is headed toward a town called Tinhead. When it arrives, the descendents of the mountain's original inhabitants attack to retake their ancestral home.

As the capital falls, Erileine discovers that Piaerre is actually the captain's son and heir, and that her teacher secretly works for the world's most powerful magician. Erileine performs magic for the first time to save her teacher's life, and then the trio escape to return to her village, where she is pressed into service as a translator for the mountain's new rulers.

Thomas arrives and reveals that retaking the mountain is only the first step in the invaders' plan. In order to stop them awakening an ancient power, Erileine and others must stop a ceremony taking place at the mountain's peak. Despite their heroism, they fail. Razi appears to fall to his death, leaving the survivors to face the unknown.
