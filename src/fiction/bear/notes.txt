## Outline

Each chapter is about 6000 words, so 15 chapters plus an epilog

### Arc

-   Act 1: setup
    -   Major: Thokmay trying to act like a prince ("the race for the throne")
    -   Major: Gifted animals' rights and comparison with challenged humans
    -   Major: Noxy and Thokmay's reunion and romantic tension
    -   Major: Shudarga returns
    -   Minor: Noxy still frightened by her growing powers
    -   Minor: Captain Jigme's return as Chaghan consul
    -   Minor: introduce Mukh
-   Act 2: the turn
    -   Major: Thokmay thinking about the balance
    -   Major: Princess Ogmin kidnapped; Mukh revealed as a villain
    -   Major: Gifted animals attacked
    -   Major: Shudarga's presence stirs up the crowd
    -   Minor: introduce Piqaluujaq
-   Act 3: finale
    -   Major: Mukh defeated, Jigme flees again
    -   Major: Rust chooses to protect Thokmay and Ogmin over her grudge
    -   Minor: Mope departs to argue animal-rights law in other kingdoms
    -   Minor: Thokmay decides to quit the race for the throne
    -   Minor: Noxy and Thokmay kiss

### Act 1: setup

#### Chapter 1: Reunion

-   Noxy is riding Cloak with Aft's herd en route to Chaghan when a tornado attacks
    -   Foreshadow Noxy keeping her distance-mind-speaking secret
-   Soldiers arrive to help
    -   One turns out to be Thokmay
    -   Awkward reunion—neither expected this
    -   Thokmay reveals tornado has targeted people who helped the garrison
-   Aft leaves Noxy behind
-   Arrive at Apple Bridge garrison
    -   Thokmay introduces Noxy to Sergeant Gyalpa
-   Mope gets in trouble with humans and hides in Thokmay's room

#### Chapter 2: Familiar Faces

-   Noxy meets Dolly (Ogmin's troll nurse) at the bath
    -   First glimpse of Thokmay's "simple" sister Ogmin
-   Dinner: Noxy meets Mope (Dolly's son, Thokmay's tutor) and Princess Ogmin
    -   Ogmin is delighted by Noxy's mountain boots
-   Consul Yisu arrives with her replacement: Captain Jigme
    -   Thokmay and Noxy recognize him as Dorbu's killer
    -   He is protected by diplomatic immunity; Thokmay is furious but contained
    -   Sergeant Gyalpa: "It's an insult, and not an accidental one"
-   Thokmay and Noxy chat on the balcony
-   Mope liberates the abused horse and delivers it to the raccoons
-   Thokmay's night: writes report to father, ghost of Edward the Dog passes through him
    -   Edward's memories: something wrong with the bridge or dam, something bad coming
    -   Foreshadow Sergeant Inambu's investigation of the Blight

## Chapter 3: The Dog Market

-   Morning: Noxy goes to the Dog Market with Princess Ogmin and Dolly
    -   Dolly faces casual prejudice from shopkeepers; Noxy is shocked
    -   Ogmin's innocent joy contrasts with the hostility around Dolly
-   Noxy learns the history of the Dog Market and Edward the Dog
    -   The dog plague came out of the Blight—foreshadow the real threat
-   Meet Mukh and Rust at the market (bear and her "assistant")
    -   Ogmin steps on Rust's paw, panic ensues, swords drawn
    -   Mukh gives Ogmin a ribbon and a "lucky" wooden ring (FIXME: ring's magic not yet clear)
-   Soldiers steer Ogmin away when a dispute breaks out in the market
    -   A farmer is found tied naked to a lamp post—his horse stolen
    -   Manpa: "Been happen along three or four times these last few months"

#### Chapter 4: Flight

-   Noxy takes Cloak flying, letting him feed and heal
    -   Spots the raccoons' barge on the Trout; foreshadow the raft plot
-   Raccoons selling the horse back into slavery
-   Bortja leads charter protesters to garrison gate: "Men over beasts!"
    -   Thokmay faces the crowd without soldiers; lets the silence work
    -   Noxy disperses the mob by making Cloak rain on them
-   The farmer whose house was destroyed is found dead
-   Service at the Old Hall for the dead farmer (Noxy stays with Cloak)
    -   Thokmay speaks: "When the tornado took him, he was riding toward home"—promises to do no less
    -   He and Noxy have a moment
-   Later, Noxy feels something old and cold with her mind

#### Chapter 5: The Tornado Returns

-   Charter mob returns; water-pump incident with raccoons and townspeople
    -   Thokmay improvises: brings everyone to garrison stable pump ("same water")
    -   Interrupted by tornado gong—the tornado has returned
-   Tornado attacks the edge of town; rider and horse killed on the road
    -   Thokmay watches from rooftop; Inambu climbs up, trembling with exhaustion
    -   Tornado retreats on its own just before Inambu has to act
-   Back at the garrison: Mope rescues two raccoons from the charter mob (wounded)
    -   Mukh brought inside to shelter; raccoons stay the night
-   That night: Mukh sneaks out via window and leads Princess Ogmin out through tunnel
    -   The wooden ring compels her to follow quietly
    -   Ogmin taken to the warehouse where Rust waits

### Act 2: the turn

#### Chapter 6: The Tornado Returns

-   Thokmay walks home from the service, runs into pump-incident mob (Mukh, raccoons again)
    -   Thokmay leads townsfolk to garrison stable pump; cuts through the anger with practical action
-   Tornado returns: kills a fleeing smuggler (town guard recognizes him from Blight-scavenging arrest)
-   Inambu defeats the tornado single-handedly on the garrison roof, but collapses from the effort
-   Princess Ogmin is missing—garrison in uproar
    -   Thokmay and Noxy briefly almost acknowledge the mind-speaking before duties intervene

#### Chapter 8: Missing!

-   Morning: Ogmin gone; search finds nothing inside the garrison
    -   Mope suspects abduction immediately
    -   Dobchang witnessed Mukh and a huge bear leaving town with a wagon after moonfall
-   Thokmay: "He even told us it was magical!" (the ring)
-   Inambu uses a sympathy spell on the ribbon Mukh gave Ogmin
    -   Reveals the princess is on the west side of Carmick Hill—in the Blight
    -   The spell costs Inambu a childhood memory (her tenth birthday party)
    -   FIXME: foreshadow that Inambu knew exactly what ribbon to track vs. the princess herself

#### Chapter 9: Into the Blight

-   Thokmay musters forty soldiers (leaves forty with Inambu) and rides out
    -   Noxy sees him off; punches his arm gently, "Blue skies"
-   March through the Blight: glass flowers, metallic grass, bloated apples, skewed distances
    -   Crosses old Pilot-era boundary markers now deep inside the Blight
-   Find Mukh waiting with two dozen smugglers at an old tree stump
    -   Mukh shows them Ogmin's cut-off braids and the ribbon—a decoy
    -   "I want you and your troops to leave Apple Bridge. By tomorrow."
    -   Sergeant Gyalpa: "We've been tricked"—Inambu's spell located the ribbon, not the princess
-   Mukh releases a tornado from a small bag using a copper spirit trap
    -   Battle chaos; tornado attacks soldiers and smugglers alike
    -   Sergeant Gyalpa breaks the spirit trap with his sword; tornado runs free

#### Chapter 10: I Know the Bear

-   Noxy stays in Apple Bridge; goes to cheer up Dolly, overhears argument between Dolly and Mope
-   Mope reluctantly reveals he knows Rust: she's a Gifted-rights activist
    -   Rust's ungifted cub was killed by hunters; courts gave her no redress
    -   Mope's own father was beaten to death by a circus trainer for protecting Mope when born gifted
    -   "That's why I taught myself everything there is to know about the law"
-   Noxy proposes: find proof Rust didn't take Ogmin (same actions as finding if she did)
    -   If Rust did it and they find Ogmin, they can balance the moral books
    -   Mope: "People rarely do their moral bookkeeping that carefully"—but agrees

### Act 3: finale

#### Chapter 11: Attacked

-   Battle in the Blight continues: freed tornado turns on everyone
    -   Gyalpa: "Fall back! We can't get past that thing!"
    -   Thokmay charges anyway; land begins to heave and churn around the spirit trap
    -   Gyalpa knocks the spirit trap loose with his sword; mirror flies and hits Gyalpa
    -   Thokmay refuses to leave Gyalpa behind: "I've already lost one sergeant"
    -   Freed tornado races toward Apple Bridge; Mukh escapes through a magic mirror

#### Chapter 12: Comes Big Song

-   Mope and Noxy in the market asking after Rust
    -   Anuat trader Piqaluujaq senses Blight magic used the previous night (his grandmother's spirit in a dreamcatcher)
    -   Edward the Dog's ghost manifests on his statue as the tornado hits Apple Bridge
-   Tornado tears through the Dog Market and garrison, destroys Edward's statue, flips river rafts
    -   Noxy and Mope sheltering in an alley; Mope saves Noxy from being lifted away
    -   Inambu defeats the tornado again from the garrison roof; collapses, borne inside
-   Warehouse by the docks collapses; Mope and Dolly rescue a man with a pinned leg
    -   The leek seller (who was rude to Dolly) is one of the rescuers
    -   Runner reports people trapped; Mope orders Noxy to find Inambu while he goes to help

#### Chapter 13: Snow

-   Thokmay and Gyalpa make their way out of the Blight on foot
    -   Thokmay splints Gyalpa's injured knee; they follow a safe road toward the Trout
    -   The Blight's strangeness: streams that trap things, bushes that flinch, snow that only Thokmay can see
    -   Thokmay's idea: douse the fire, reducing the land's anger; Gyalpa throws him a rope to navigate back
-   They emerge near a ruined mill on the Trout; Rust ambushes them
    -   Rust breaks their swords; sends Bucket (the horse) fleeing
    -   Rust has Ogmin on a raft; Ogmin is unharmed—Rust made her a straw doll
    -   Gyalpa ordered off the raft; Thokmay refuses to abandon him until Thokmay overrides himself
    -   FIXME: Thokmay and Rust's conversation about animal rights needs setup in earlier chapters

#### Chapter 14: The Other Side of the Mirror

-   Noxy goes to Inambu's room to report; finds it empty
    -   Dolly arrives; Mukh comes through the magic mirror
    -   Noxy hides under a sheet-covered dresser; overhears Mukh and Inambu
    -   Mukh is Inambu's brother; the spirit trap is lost (Gyalpa destroyed it)
    -   Plan: Inambu will send soldiers away, then break the dam herself
    -   Inambu's motivation: the spirit trap let magic take from the recipient—no more personal cost
    -   "Justice is worth whatever it costs"
-   Mukh departs back through the mirror; Inambu prepares to tie up Noxy
    -   Dolly bursts in and carries Noxy out through the window via the apple tree
-   Meanwhile: Thokmay on the raft challenges Rust about her motives
    -   Rust explains the cages that animals live in ("twenty years trading, still need permission each time")
    -   Rust on her son: "killing ungifted animals isn't murder"
    -   Thokmay realizes Rust couldn't bring herself to kill Ogmin (the doll gives it away)

#### Chapter 15: A Trap Is Sprung

-   Noxy and Dolly see Inambu send the soldiers out to be ambushed with a false vision
    -   Can't go to the soldiers directly; Inambu could silence them with magic
    -   Find Mope at the warehouse rescue; Dolly and Noxy tell him everything
-   Noxy flies Cloak out and spots the smugglers' ambush in the trees
    -   Warns the soldiers just in time; the soldiers rout the smugglers
    -   Mukh escapes with his one magic boot (twenty-yard strides); Noxy can't keep up
-   Noxy returns to tell soldiers the truth about Inambu; soldier doesn't believe her
    -   Noxy flies back to Apple Bridge
-   Mope and Dolly at the dam: Inambu is chanting, blue light building
    -   Mope throws stones, distracts her; stand-off with Inambu's sword
    -   Something breaks through into the Trout—Blight magic released
    -   Edward the Dog's ghost attacks Inambu; she wounds it; it spends itself in a final leap
    -   Dolly steps up behind Inambu and knocks her out
    -   Edward fades: "Humans—they just don't understand…"

#### Chapter 16: The Bear's Choice

-   On the raft: Mukh finds them, wants Ogmin as a hostage to flee to Barra Bantang
    -   "I could carry them both if I'd ever found the other magic boot"
    -   Rust: "She's mine!" (Ogmin is her bargaining chip with the king)
    -   Mukh draws a snake-tongue magic knife; tells Thokmay to tie Rust up
    -   Thokmay refuses; Mukh lunges—Rust charges and tackles Mukh into the dark Blightwater
    -   Thokmay throws Rust a rope; it snaps; Rust and Mukh are pulled under and gone
-   The Blightwater floods the little island; strange creatures circle
    -   Thokmay despairs—Ogmin can't swim the Blightwater
    -   Cloak arrives with Noxy and Gyalpa; cloud won't descend into Blightwater
    -   Gyalpa lowers a rope; Thokmay ties it to Ogmin; both hang on as Cloak tows them to shore
    -   Thokmay's heels splash in the Blight; they scramble up the bank
    -   Ogmin: "I really want to go home."

#### Epilog: The End of the Letter

-   Days later: Thokmay writes to his father
    -   Item 1: Mope is leaving to argue animal-rights law in other kingdoms
    -   Item 2: Noxy given the freedom of Loghan (Thokmay's signet ring, turned into a bracelet)
    -   Item 3: Thokmay is returning to the capital with Ogmin
-   Noxy and Thokmay's real goodbye: he waves as Cloak climbs into the sky, "breathless at how soft her lips had been"
-   Inambu has woken with a blank mind; Ogmin is teaching her about dolls ("Lily or Rose?")
-   Thokmay signs his letter "Your dutiful and loving son"—first time he has written "loving"
-   Final line: "there are many more stories to tell before that one"

## World

-   Loghan: one of the Ninety Kingdoms, setting for both books
-   Apple Bridge: a prosperous river town on the Trout, downstream from the Blight
    -   FIXME: better names than "Apple Bridge" and "the Trout"
    -   The Dog Market: Apple Bridge's famous open-air market, named after Edward the Dog
    -   The dam: holds back things floating downriver from the Blight
    -   The Old Hall: the town's witness hall for namings, weddings, and remembrances
-   The Blight: the dumping ground for the Pilots' leftover and failed magic
    -   Fast Lake: at the center; a year may pass per normal day; contains magic that works in reverse
    -   Safe roads: built by the Pilots to carry things in; still exist, somewhat
-   Chaghan: neighboring kingdom; politically hostile; chose Jigme as consul to insult Loghan
-   Garheim, Former Tongsa, Latter Tongsa, Barra Bantang, Anuvatik: other countries mentioned
-   Stale: home for the cloudherds
