# The Princess and the Bear

## World

-   Loghan: one of the Ninety Kingdoms, setting for both books
-   Ghorahj: a prosperous river town on the Sochla River, downstream from the Blight
    -   The Dog Market: Ghorahj's famous open-air market, named after Eddard the Dog
    -   The dam: holds back things floating downriver from the Blight
    -   The Old Hall: the town's witness hall for namings, weddings, and remembrances
    -   The garrison house: local center of royal authority
-   The Blight: the dumping ground for the Pilots' leftover and failed magic
    -   Fast Lake: at the center; a year may pass per normal day; contains magic that works in reverse
    -   Safe roads: built by the Pilots to carry things in; still exist, somewhat
-   Chaghan: neighboring kingdom; politically hostile; chose Yangyal as consul to insult Loghan
-   Garheim, Former Tongsa, Latter Tongsa, Barra Bantang, Anuvatik: other countries mentioned
-   Stale: home for the cloudherds

## Characters

-   Thokmay (m)
    -   PoV protagonist (along with Noxy)
    -   Struggling between what's expected of him (become a prince) and what he wants
    -   Character development: decides to walk away from the race for the throne to become a Balance
-   Noxy (Probably Noxious) (f)
    -   PoV protagonist (along with Thokmay)
    -   Can mind-speak at distance, mind-speaking with humans (FIXME: no nosebleeds: over-used trope)
    -   Still hiding her powers from other cloudherds
    -   Character development: FIXME
-   Princess Ogmin (f)
    -   Thokmay's older sister; developmentally simple; sweet-natured
    -   Her vulnerability is both a plot driver and a lens on how Loghan treats Gifted animals
    -   The latter has to be handled *very* carefully
-   Dolly (f)
    -   Gifted troll (gorilla); Ogmin's nursemaid; Mope's mother
    -   Bears casual prejudice with quiet dignity; fierce when her son is threatened
-   The Learned Mope (m)
    -   Gifted troll; Thokmay's tutor in law and history; Dolly's son
    -   Reveal: his (ungifted) father died defending him; Mope turned that grief into legal expertise
    -   Character development: decides to leave Thokmay's service to argue animal-rights law across the kingdoms
-   Sergeant Gyalpa (m)
    -   Thokmay's head soldier; deeply competent; impassive
    -   Becomes more approachable when stranded with the prince in the Blight
-   Sergeant Inambu (f)
    -   The troop's magician
    -   Reveal: Mukh's sister; their home was swallowed by the Blight
    -   Wants the power to do magic without personal cost (the spirit trap offers this)
-   Cloak (m)
    -   Noxy's young cloud
-   Mukh (m)
    -   This story's villain
    -   Has one magic boot that lets him leap twenty yards at a stride
    -   Seeks access to Fast Lake for its reverse magic ("takes from the recipient")
    -   Is using his sister: she is idealistic, he is not
-   Rust (f)
    -   Gifted bear trader; has sold in Ghorahj for twenty years
    -   Her ungifted cub was killed by hunters; courts refused justice
    -   Kidnaps Ogmin as political leverage (egged on by Mukh) but cannot bring herself to harm her
-   Yangyal (m)
    -   A Chaghan spy in Stale in the first book
    -   Now Chaghan's consul in Ghorahj; diplomatically untouchable
    -   His appointment is a calculated insult from Chaghan
    -   Turns out to be in an alliance of convenience with Mukh (who has outsmarted Yangyal)
    -   Not a believer in Free Law (no fan of Shudarga's) or of equal rights for the Gifted
    -   Escapes justice in the end (will show up in the final book of the trilogy)
-   Eddard the Dog (ghost) (m)
    -   Richest animal in the world before the dog plague; built the Gifted school in the capital
    -   His ghost has been growing stronger rather than fading because something is feeding it
    -   FIXME: what is feeding it?
-   Learned Shudarga (f)
    -   A distant cousin of the king whose writing about Free Law sparked a rebellion
    -   She fled to Choghan, but is no longer welcome there and has been sent back to stir up trouble in Loghan
    -   She is *not* working with Yangyal or Mukh, but the prince, Noxy, and Muck suspect she is
-   Bortje (f)
    -   A leek seller in Ghorahj who thinks the Gifted don't deserve full legal rights
    -   An opportunist driven by adulation as much as by ideology
    -   FIXME: how does she fit in?
-   Aft (m)
    -   The new reeve of Stale; Noxy's chaperone on the trip to Chaghan
-   Sour and Testy: two other cloudherds

## Problems

-   Noxy doesn't develop much in this story
    -   She has turned down the forest, but doesn't know who to be next
    -   FIXME: her extra abilities aren't as strong as they were, and are fading, so she has regrets
    -   Could try to become reeve like her mother, but that feels small
    -   Granna Fee has passed away (Grappa Gas is lost without her)
    -   Accepts that she doesn't have to follow in her mother's footsteps
-   Build the alliance between Yangyal and Mukh
-   Third book has to end with the Flying Mountain going off course, so need to mention it here
-   The raccoons are selling "freed" animals back into slavery
-   What is the old, tired thing Noxy senses?
-   Show that Mukh controls the tornado
    -   The smuggler it killed presumably knew
-   Edward the Dog's ghost: what is giving him strength, and what disaster is he warning about?
-   What is Yangyal's reason for being in Ghorahj?
-   Reveal that the extra weigh Noxy senses is Ogmin's ring
-   The 10-year-old burning nest at the Blight's edge is mentioned and then dropped
-   What happens to/with Shudarga
-   What happens to Bortja and her mob
-   The ring's enchantment logic: Mukh tells the princess to wear the
    ring for a week and then throw it in a well — but the ring compels
    her to leave on the same night it is given; the "wear it a week"
    story is part of the ruse, but this may need to be clarified so
    readers understand the ring began working immediately
-   Raccoons' continuity: The barge raccoons, the garrison raccoons, and
    the warehouse raccoons all appear to be the same pair, but this is
    never made explicit; if so, Mukh's raccoons had already
    infiltrated the garrison area before the kidnapping, which is a
    plot point that could use a clearer signal

## Outline

### Chapter 1

-   Tornado attacks Noxy and other cloudherds
    -   She lands at Ghorahj, reunites with Thokmay
    -   Injured Cloak needs time to heal, so Noxy stays while Aft continues to Chaghan to pick up a passenger
-   Tornado has struck three times, always targeting people who helped the garrison
    -   Thokmay believes it came out of a bottle retrieved from the Blight
-   Mope stirs up trouble baiting a chai seller who doesn't like the Gifted
    -   Takes refuge in the prince's room

### Chapter 2

-   Noxy meets Princess Ogmin, Dolly, and Mope at dinner
-   Yangyal arrives as Chaghan consul: diplomatic immunity makes him untouchable
-   Mope helps some raccoons, a bear, and a crow rescue an ungifted horse
-   Thokmay is writing a report to his father when Eddard the Dog's ghost appears in his room
    -   Leaves him shivering with premonition about the bridge, dam, and Blight

### Chapter 3

-   Sergeant Inambu reports on her visit to Blight to Thokmay
    -   He asks her to spy on Yangyal, but they agree to defer because of the spell's cost
-   Noxy goes to the market with Ogmin and Dolly
    -   They meet Rust and Mukh
    -   Mukh gives Ogmin a glowing wooden ring
-   Noxy spots a barge on the river piloted by two raccoons with no human crew

### Chapter 4

-   Noxy takes Cloak up for a flight
    -   Sees raccoons sailing a barge
    -   Noxy turns back without seeing the raccoons sell the horse back into slavery
-   Mob led by Bortje has formed outside the garrison house gates
    -   Horse's owner complaining: "People over beasts!"
    -   Noxy disperses the mob by raining on them
-   The missing farmer from the opening is found dead
    -   Funeral service that evening
-   Afterward, Noxy senses something old and tired in the Blight

### Chapter 5

-   Prince sees mob harassing raccoons for using a water pump
    -   Wants to force them to get water from the stable
    -   Sergeant Gyalpa tries to dissuade him, but the prince is angry
-   Tornado returns
    -   It kills a Blight smuggler who had escaped custody
    -   Sergeant Inambu collapses after apparently turning it back from the town
-   Mope arrives, having rescueed the raccoons from Bortja's mob
-   Aft returns carrying Learned Shudarga, who announces she has come to surrender

### Chapter 6

-   Thokmay puts Shudarga in detention in the guest suite of the garrison house
    -   She has brought printed copies of all of Loghan's laws, and more are being smuggled in
-   Noxy has a run-in with the same chai seller that Moep argued with
    -   She then visits Aft, who says he's leaving and she can meet them in four days
    -   And bumps into Yangyal on her way back to the garrison house
-   Noxy takes Ogmin and Mope up for a ride
    -   Some backstory about things from the Blight being caught by the bridge
    -   Noxy senses an invisible extra passenger on Cloak
-   That night, Ogmin's ring compels her to leave with the raccoons
    -   Mukh and Rust are waiting, and kidnap her

### Chapter 7

-   Confusion the next morning when the everyone realizes that Princess Ogmin is gone
    -   A witness saw a bear hauling a wagon after moonfall
    -   Inambu's spell appears to locate Ogmin at the Blight's edge
-   Thokmay accuses Yangyal, who swears innocence on his own blood
-   Mope questions Shudarga with Noxy looking on
    -   Turns into a debate about Gifted rights
-   Thokmay prepares to ride into the Blight
-   Vignette: Ogmin is with Rust and the raccoons
    -   The latter are uncomfortable keeping her
-   Ends with Thokmay writing a difficult letter to his father
