# Eimin

- *Eimin* is a YA fantasy novella about a teenage boy trying to find his place in the world.
  - 32,000 words / 20 chapters.
- Most people in Eimin's village (Medef) are Aphsi (empaths): they can *drome* the feelings of others.
  - But Eimin is *nif* (the equivalent of deaf), which makes him feel socially awkward.
    - Almost all *nif* are male.
  - Being *nif* is a metaphor for being on the spectrum and feeling that you don't understand people.
    - Also for being deaf.
  - The story should appeal to bookish kids who feel that they never quite fit in.
- The story is told in the first person from Eimin's point of view.
  - Occasional asides give the impression that he is telling the story to the reader as an adult.

## Major Characters

- Eimin (protagonist): male, 16, born and raised in Medef (more below).
  - An only child: his father left the family when he was a child.
  - Eimin blames himself, even though his mother has told him that she and his father just fell out of love.
  - Wants Hediyeh and despises himself for it.
    - She's the alpha female in his age cohort, and wanting what he can't have is a way of punishing himself.
    - Recognizing this and outgrowing it is part of his story arc.
  - Finds out he's being trained by Razi to join the Other Hand (*nif* who protect the Aphsi from outsiders).
    - The Aphsi can't kill because of their empathy.
  - Development: comes to accept himself as he is.
- Razi (ally): an outsider (and therefore *nif*) who visits Medef several times a year.
  - He is one of Lady Kembe's agents (does the practical things she can't).
  - Becomes Eimin's teacher, but we later discover that he is training Eimin to be part of the Other Hand.
  - Razi doesn't know how old he is or where he comes from
    - He suspects this is a result of some spell.
- Hediyeh (antagonist): female, also 16, born and raised in Medef.
  - Enjoys other people's hurt the way some people enjoy the taste of ripe cheese.
  - Knows that Eimin has a crush on her, but dismisses him both because he's *nif* and because she's mean.
- Suresh (antagonist): male, early 20s, a traveling printer and bookseller.
  - Initially friendly to both Eimin and Hediyeh, but alienates Eimin by growing closer to Hediyeh.
  - Turns out that he wants to sacrifice Hediyeh to get magical power.

## Minor Characters

- Eats Three Times (antagonist): female Gifted lion.
  - a background presence who is only encountered once in person.
- Huda: Eimin's mother, doing her best to understand and comfort a difficult teenage boy.
- Jephel (male, teenager): placid *nif* a couple of years older than Eimin who is apprenticed to Yassen.
  - Already accepts himself in a way that Eimin both despises and envies (foil for Eimin).
- Blue: a Gifted (intelligent) goat who manages the village flock.
  - Isn't bothered by the unintelligent ones being slaughtered as long as it's humane.
  - Comments that it gives him a chance to make sure the Gifted ones *aren't* harmed.
- Lady Kembe (female): magician in Ossisswe (mentioned but never encountered).
- Dudir: caravaneer (young, abortive romantic interest / target for Hediyeh).
  - Actually a bandit.
- Balwir: head caravaneer, Dudir's father.
  - Also actually a bandit.
- Yassen the butcher (*nif*).
- Kirash: Yassen's brother and a heavy drinker (also *nif*).
  - Sides with the bandits out of resentment.
- Layla and Mureya: Hediyeh's friends.
  - Mureya is in a relationship with Jephel, in part to break away from Hediyeh.
  - Hediyeh doesn't tease her about it because she can't get a rise out of either Hediyeh or Jephel.
- Granna Ferouz: Medef's weather wife.

## More About the Setting

- Medef was a minor port on an inlet in Lake Karaband before the Disputation.
  - Lake Karaband became the Sea of Glass.
  - Ships, fish, etc. trapped in the glass, bits above the surface eroded away.
  - Glass itself is now cloudy, but here and there things can still be seen.
  - Cracks in the glass filled with windblown dirt and a few hardy plants.
- The Pilots' palace (actually just a small summer house by their standards).
  - Everything built to a larger-than-human scale.
  - Home to ghosts: nobody sleeps near it (or goes near it at all unless they're desperate or brave).
  - Voices tempting people inside…
- Magicians must trade parts of themselves for power.
  - Eimin makes a bitter comment about getting nothing in exchange for being *nif*.
  - Razi chides him: most people in the world don't either.
- The ghost in Suresh's ring guides him to what he thinks is power.
  - Actually a human magician wanting to be released after being trapped for a thousand years.
- The Pilots could trade parts of other people.
  - People have been trying to regain that power since their fall.
  - Foreshadows the big reveal in a later book: humans are magic fuel
    - The Pilots' Disputation was about the morality of that.
- Suresh lies to Eimin about magicians creating *nif* for the Other Hand.
  - Eimin's realization that his mother wouldn't do that to him is his character turning point.
  - As is his realization that Razi *would* do this.
- People can drome that Suresh is going to betray Hediyeh, but they think that means he's going to move on.
  - Hediyeh enjoys practicing being forlorn.
- Kirash's drunkenness is a warning to Eimin and Jephel.

## Outline

### Chapter 1

Eimin, a Karabandi listener, describes his childhood in Medef,
where most people are Aphsi who can *drome* emotions.
He is *nif* (equivalent of deaf) and cannot,
which leaves him isolated and occasionally bullied by other Aphsi children,
particularly a girl his own age named Hediyeh.
A cruel prank brings him closer to Razi,
an older *nif* who reveals that he too was once bullied.
Razi teaches Eimin that careful attention and planning can compensate for the lack of *droming*,
then offers to train Eimin.

### Chapter 2

Five years later, Eimin is Razi's apprentice,
and joins him on a hunt to for gargoyles who are pillaging Medef livestock.
(Mention Blue but do not introduce him.)
Razi acts as bait,
giving Eimin a chance to kill one of the gargoyles.
Eimin saves Razi,
but falls and hurts his wrist,
which spoils the victory for him.

### Chapter 3

Eimin and Razi return to Medef as evening falls,
passing through the lemon grove (Eimin comments on "my father's tree").
They encounter Blue,
who is obviously an old friend of Razi's;
they give Blue the good news about the gargoyles,
but Blue teases Eimin about falling.
Back in the village,
Eimin sees Jephel and Mureya,
(contrast Aphsi emotional awareness and Jephel's placidity
with his own feelings of social clumsiness).
Early the next morning,
he woken when news arrives that a caravan has been attacked near Medef
Eats Three Times' bandits.
Razi goes to guide the caravan in,
but orders Eimin to stay behind.
After a warning from Blue,
Eimin, Yassen, and Jephel fend off an abortive attempt by the lion and her bandits
to steal some of Medef's goats.

### Chapter 4

The caravan arrives in Medef.
(Comment that small caravans like this are not common:
Medef sees two big caravans a year plus couriers and other travelers.)
As the villagers welcome it,
Eimin notices Hediyeh eyeing Dudir,
the brawny son of the caravan leader.
Another caravaneer, Suresh, turns out to have a small printing press
as well as books, paper, and brushes.
He and Eimin strike up a conversation,
during which we find out that Suresh is only a couple of years older than Eimin and Hediyeh.
Suresh talks about the Sea of Glass (it's the first time he's seen it).

### Chapter 5

The next morning,
Eimin walks out to the Pilots' Palace to sit under his father's tree.
He has a brief exchange with Blue,
who thanks him for fending off the bandits yesterday.
(Eimin asks if Blue is bothered by the Aphsi eating meat,
and Blue says, "You get used to it,"
and explains that his job lets him make sure that other Gifted goats aren't harmed.)
Eimin finds Suresh studying the waterstone
(comment about Suresh fingering a ring on a necklace and tucking it into his shirt).
They talk about the waterstone,
which keeps Medef alive,
and then about drawing,
during which Suresh mentions that he had a conversation with Hediyeh the previous evening.
Eimin reacts,
making Suresh ask if Eimin is interested in her,
which makes Eimin feel socially uncomfortable again.
He asks if Suresh is,
and Suresh says that she reminds him of someone.
Eimin returns home to find Razi and two strangers waiting for him.
They drug his tea and ask him questions,
after which Razi explains who the Other Hand are
and that he has been training Eimin to join them.

### Chapter 6

Eimin goes to visit the traders.
Hediyeh and her clique are talking to Dudir;
Suresh comments to Eimin that Dudir doesn't realize
the girls can tell he is lying about the places he has been and things he has done.
Eimin says this to Dudir,
who becomes belligerent.
It turns into a shoving match,
which makes Layla, Mureya, and other Aphsi uncomfortable,
but excites Hediyeh.
Eimin doesn't fight the way Razi taught him;
instead,
he returns home,
where Huda talks about his father and their courtship.
They end the day by going up to the roof to look at the stars.
(Comment here about how some of them are wanderers,
which makes Eimin dream of going to other worlds.)

### Chapter 7

Granna Ferouz wakes Eimin and Huda to warn them that a storm is coming
(and that they're steadily getting worse,
which foreshadows events in a later story).
Eimin helps Jephel get the posting yard ready,
then takes shelter with the caravaneers in the hostel.
Kirash comes into the hostel to say that he's seen some suspicious characters.
Razi, Eimin, and Yassen fend off two bandits,
only to discover that the attack was a distraction
to get the three of them out of the hostel.
Balwir and a few other caravaneers are actually bandits working with Eats Three Times,
and used the time to subdue the other villagers
and set a trap for Razi and Yassen.

### Chapter 8

The bandits easily subdue Yassen but Razi fights back.
Razi is injured,
but Eimin and Suresh escape.
Suresh comments that what he's doing is too important to be trapped by mere bandits (foreshadowing).
They make their way through the storm to the palace
to pick up Eimin's emergency gear (part of Razi's training).
Suresh asks if Eimin has any way of contacting Razi's friends.
Eimin isn't sure if he means the Other Hand or not,
but since he doesn't either way,
he says "no".
Suresh looks relieved.
Eimin sees the stone in the ring on Suresh's necklace start to glow,
but then they hear voices and believe the bandits have found them.

### Chapter 9

They go hunting for the voices,
but find Hediyeh, Layla, and Mureya in the Palace.
They were avoiding chores,
but miscalculated and were trapped when the storm hit.
(They dromed Eimin and Suresh,
and were going to join them,
but then dromed something that scared them;
we will realize later that it was Suresh's ring,
but for now just comment on ghost stories.)
The girls are taken aback by Eimin's weaponry.
Suresh explains what's going on,
and flirts a little with Hediyeh.
When Eimin makes a remark about this to him out of earshot of the girls,
Suresh asks why Eimin hasn't flirted with her himself,
since she's obviously interested in him.
While Eimin is trying to figure out how to respond to that,
Mureya rushes in to tell them that bandits are approaching.

### Chapter 10

Suresh and Eimin capture one of the bandits with help from the girls' droming,
but the other gets away.
The way the bandit looks at the girls makes them so uncomfortable
that Mureya and Layla have to go to another room.
They interrogate their captive using Hediyeh's droming to tell when he's lying,
and discover that the point of taking over Medef is to ambush a traveler
who stole something in Ossisswe.
The bandit who escaped is going to bring reinforcements,
so they need to flee.
The captive bandit tries to escape;
Suresh uses magic to prevent him,
then kills him as the spell starts to wear off,
which makes Hediyeh nauseous.

### Chapter 11

Eimin confronts Suresh about his use of  magic.
Suresh explains that he started training to be a magician in Ossisswe
but found the price too high:
a fellow apprentice who made a poor bargain,
and Suresh saw what happened.
He then wonders what it would have been like to be a Pilot,
and to use other people to pay the price of spells.
(That's why human magic is so much smaller than theirs was:
it's limited to what a single person can muster.)
Suresh then goes to comfort Hediyeh;
for a moment we see her as a vulnerable, frightened teenager,
but she recovers her poise.
Suresh says that he had to do what he did to,
and promises that he will only ever do what he has to (foreshadowing).
Eimin goes to check on Mureya and Layla.
Mureya has also been nauseous because of the killing,
and says that she felt something horrible when it happened (foreshadowing).
Layla didn't feel as much,
but is angry and makes scathing remarks about the *nif*.
Blue shows up to fetch them,
saying that Razi has a plan
("Razi *always* has a plan").

### Chapter 12

Blue and Eimin go back to the hostel
to negotiate for the safety of the girls.
Blue tells Balwir that they keep a stash of good liquor for important visitors.
When Kirash says it's the first he's heard of it,
Yassen says of course it is:
if Kirash knew about it,
he'd have drunk it all.
Yassen gets the liquor out of its hiding place.
Eats Three Times is suspicious,
so Yassen drinks first.
All of the bandits drink except Eats Three Times,
who says that cats don't drink,
and Dudir,
whose father forbids it.
Dinner arrives;
Blue tastes it to prove it isn't poisoned.
When the bandits eat it,
it reacts with the drug in the liquor to knock them out.
Eats Three Time takes a swipe at Blue and then escapes.
Dudir attacks Eimin.

### Chapter 13

Eimin defeats Dudir,
almost killing him before Razi intervenes.
Razi angrily tells Eimin he would have sorted things out given a little more time.
Eimin asks, "Like you saved my father?"
We then get the back story of caravaneers brawling in Medef,
taking advantage of the fact that the Aphsi wouldn't fight back,
and of Razi acting just a little too slowly to save Eimin's father.
Razi says he has regretted that every single day.
They take the bandits to the edge of town and give them each a mule but no provisions.
They will be tracked by an Aphsi to ensure they actually leave.
Balwir says it's a death sentence,
but Kirash says he'll show them how to survive:
he has chosen to go with them rather than stay where he isn't wanted.

### Chapter 14

Eimin speaks with Suresh,
who says that Lady Kembe creates *nif* in exchange for the Other Hand's services.
He says that if Eimin will help him,
he can use the power of the waterstone to make Eimin like other Aphsi
and prove what Lady Kembe has been doing.

### Chapter 15

Eimin hikes out onto the Sea of Glass to clear his head.
He decides that Suresh is telling the truth.

### Chapter 16

Eimin returns to Medef and tells Suresh he believes him.
Suresh reveals that he is the traveler that the bandits were looking for,
and that his ring will let him unlock the power of the waterstone.
Suresh says it will be worth one more sacrifice (foreshadowing)
but he needs Eimin's help to make sure Razi doesn't intervene.
Suresh then goes off to find Hediyeh.
After he leaves,
Mureya comes to Eimin and says that something feels wrong about Suresh,
but Eimin doesn't believe her.
Instead,
he uses the drugs that were used to knock out the bandits
to knock out Razi.

Eimin goes out to the lemon grove to be alone with his guilty feelings,
but overhears his mother talking to Blue under his father's tree.
Eimin realizes that Suresh was lying.

### Chapter 18

Eimin runs to the palace,
where he sees Suresh and Hediyeh kissing at the gate.
He slips inside in time to see Suresh lure Hediyeh into the waterstone's pool.
Suresh slips on his ring, which possesses him.
Eimin jumps into the pool and struggle with Suresh
as he tries to sacrifice Hediyeh to the waterstone.
Eimin jams Hediyeh's fingers into Suresh's mouth to stop his spell.
The ring goes dark and Suresh flees.
Eimin wakes on the pool's edge to find Razi bandaging Hediyeh.

### Epilog

In the month that follows,
Eimin mostly sleeps and watches the world from the rooftop
while Hediyeh becomes the hero of the story in the village.    
Razi visits regularly,
bringing the two members of the Other Hand,
who debrief Eimin in detail.
Razi reports that Suresh has vanished without a trace,
eluding even a spell cast by Lady Kembe.
He then presents Eimin with a chessboard.
