# The Cloudherd and the Tiger's Boy

-   Aim for 90,000 words (similar to *Iffy* and *The Bookster's Apprentice*)
    -   I have the time, and that's a better length for this age group
-   Need a clear story arc with a clear ending
    -   Must lead into *The Princess and the Bear* and *The Bookster's Apprentice*
-   Not too many characters or too many plot twists
    -   *The Last Wolf* is far too complicated…
-   Theme: good people can do bad things for good reasons
    -   And you can find yourself allied with people you detest

## Backstory

-   Is Noxy's father still alive (just not himself)?
    -   Yes: Alva speaks for the trolls, but Ulfmaerr is still the forest's human
-   Shudarga is a scholar who believes the law should belong to everyone
    -   Threatens the established order because royalty get income / maintain control by renting out laws
-   Dorbu and his unit have taken her side
    -   Thokmay is caught up without a way to escape
-   Dorbu is a very minor magician
    -   "I know a few tricks"
    -   Callback to Ulfmaerr, who bargained his self away to the forest to save the village from plague
-   The soldiers that the Stalers rescue reluctantly take over the village when Shudarga arrives
    -   She is trying to escape to Garheim
-   Nangyal is an incompetent magician
    -   Arrogance is born out of resentment
    -   Family has money: he believes in the existing order of rented laws
    -   Noxy and Thokmay find themselves allied with him even though he detests them and vice versa
-   Simultaneously, the forest wants Noxy to become its voice
    -   Alva was Ulfmaerr's replacement, but is getting old
    -   Dorbu sacrifices himself and takes on that burden for Shudarga's sake
-   Three acts:
    -   Soldiers are rescued, Noxy becomes suspicious of them and of Thokmay
        -   Build tension about Noxy's headaches and the forest
        -   Ends with soldiers taking over and Thokmay revealing himself to Noxy and Nangyal
    -   Soldiers in control, villagers start to resist
        -   Ends with Shudarga's arrival
    -   Shudarga is reluctantly ruthless
        -   Contemptuous of Nangyal, but argues with Granna Fee as an equal
-   Get rid of the lightnings: one too many things
    -   Ending is Dorbu calling the forest to attack the rebels *and* the Chaghan forces

## World

-   Loghan: one of the Ninety Kingdoms, and the setting for the story
    -   Called Gandan in current version, but that's too close to Garheim, so rename
-   Chaghan: neighboring kingdom which is backing Shudarga's rebellion for its own reasons

## Characters

-   Noxy (Probably Noxious): PoV protagonist
    -   Smart but headstrong: always thinks she knows what's best for people
    -   Rationalizes her mistakes and browbeats her friends into going along with her
    -   Idolizes her dead father
    -   Character development: realizes that she can be wrong sometimes and that mistakes have consequences
-   Sensy
    -   Noxy's friend, the only girl of her own age in the village
    -   Has always measured herself against Noxy and come up short in her own mind
    -   Crush on Rash
    -   Character development: learns to stand up for herself against Noxy
        -   "He didn't ask me to go to the fancy.  *I* asked *him*."
-   Rash
    -   Noxy's friend, the only boy of similar age in the village
    -   Cocky
    -   Crush on Sensy
    -   Character development: outgrows his cockiness
-   Indy (Literally Indigestible): Noxy's mother
    -   Smart, a natural leader
    -   Leery of getting close to people after Cough's death
        -   He was the one person she couldn't wrap around her finger (Granna Fee's comment)
    -   Starts to fall for Sergeant Dorbu
-   Granna Fee: Indy's mother / Noxy's grandmother
    -   The village schoolteacher
    -   She is seeing Grappa Gas, but doesn't take it too seriously
-   Grappa Gas: Aft's father / Granna Fee's suitor
    -   Brave, full of tall tales, one-legged, a bit of comic relief but fierce when angered
    -   He raised Aft on his own
    -   More serious about Granna Fee than she is about him
-   Aft (Disgusting Aftertaste): Grappa Gas's son / Rind's husband
    -   Big and strong, religious, fiercely protective of his wife Rind and the baby that's on its way
    -   Argues with his father about everything
-   Rind
    -   Aft's very pregnant wife
    -   Originally from Duck Droppings and would like to move back
        -   Another thing for Aft and Gas to argue about
-   Nangyal: Stale's *tralpa* (government representative) and the villain
    -   An incompetent magician, is made fun of by the villagers
    -   FIXME: add back in his magical incompetence
-   Sergeant Dorbu
    -   Handsome, a natural leader, idealistic
    -   A rebel: a true believer in Shudarga's cause
    -   Starts to fall for Indy
-   Thokmay
    -   A prince (member of the extended royal family, possible contender for the throne)
    -   Identity is a secret: was placed in the regiment to learn common ways, couldn't escape
    -   Character development: his time among the soldiers has made him uncomfortable about his privilege
        -   He haltingly explains his realizations to Noxy, who resents the analogies he makes with her privilege
-   Kulbinder
    -   A Gifted tiger, Thokmay's fiercely loyal bodyguard
-   Cough: Noxy's father
    -   Was the *tralpa*, but died during a plague (used his magic to save people)
-   The Herd of Trees: a conscious forest (backdrop)
    -   Wants Noxy to be its voice in the world
-   Alva: leader of the local trolls (gifted chimpanzees)
    -   The current voice of the forest, originally from Garheim
-   Miscellaneous other villagers and soldiers
    -   Most of Stale's adults are away rounding up the clouds during the story
