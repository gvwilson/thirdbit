--- layout: post title: "Giving Ourselves Permission" date: 2011-02-18 17:54:17 --- There's a line from Alan Furst's 1930s-era spy novel Dark Star that has stuck with me: "We need people who can do good, and not get caught." It came to mind again today as I read this WSJ article on social impact bonds: at one level it's inspiring, but at another, it feels like we're trying to give ourselves permission to do good by wedging it into market economics.
