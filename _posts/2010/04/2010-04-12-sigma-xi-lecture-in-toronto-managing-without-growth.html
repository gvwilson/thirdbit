--- layout: post title: "Sigma Xi Lecture in Toronto: Managing Without Growth" date: 2010-04-12 13:33:44 --- Without Growth. Slower by Design, Not Disaster Prof. Peter Victor Environmental Studies York University Wednesday, April 14, 2010, 1:00pm Senior Combination Room, Trinity College 6 Hoskin Avenue University of Toronto Economic growth is the over-arching policy objective of governments worldwide. Yet its long-term viability is increasingly questioned because of environmental impacts and impending and actual shortages of energy and material resources. Furthermore, rising incomes in rich countries bear little relation to gains in happiness and well-being. Growth has not eliminated poverty, brought full employment or protected the environment. Results from a simulation model of the Canadian economy suggest that it is possible to have full employment, eradicate poverty, reduce greenhouse gas emissions and maintain fiscal balance without economic growth. It's time to turn our attention away from pursuing growth and towards specific objectives more directly relating to our well-being and that of the planet. All students, faculty, and the general public are welcome.
