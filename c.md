# Proposed Blog Category Labels

**`programming`** - Writing code, algorithms, implementation details, language-specific content. Replaces most uses of the catch-all `software` label. Include discussion of software design and testing.

**`tools`** - Editors, version control systems, build tools, developer environments. For posts primarily about the tools programmers use rather than the code they write.

**`data-science`** - Data analysis, statistics, visualization, machine learning. For posts aimed at or about the data science community and its practices.

**`education`** - Pedagogy, instructional methods, learning theory, classroom practice. For posts about *how* to teach, regardless of subject. Includes curriculum and assessment.

**`software-carpentry`** - Posts specifically about the Software Carpentry / Carpentries initiative: workshops, instructor training, governance. Replaces `swcarpentry`. Includes general posts about scientific computing.

**`cs-education`** - Computer science education research and practice: how CS is taught at the K-12 and university levels, what students learn and struggle with.

**`student-projects`** - Capstone and consulting courses, supervising student projects, post-mortems on student work. More precise than the current `students` label.

**`empirical-se`** - Evidence-based software engineering: studies of how programmers work, what tools and practices actually help, and what the data show.

**`research-methods`** - Study design, methodology, reproducibility, open science. For posts about *how* research is (or should be) conducted.

**`open-source`** - Free and open source software philosophy, licensing, contribution culture, governance of open projects.

**`conferences`** - Conference reports, talk summaries, event recaps. For posts whose primary content is reporting on or reflecting on a specific event.

**`diversity`** - Inclusion, equity, gender and race in tech, representation in publishing and hiring. Distinct from general `society` because it focuses on the tech community specifically.

**`academia`** - University culture, academic careers, peer review, graduate school, the politics of research institutions.

**`community-events`** - for DemoCamp and similar events.

**`ethics`** - Computing ethics, privacy, surveillance, AI ethics, professional responsibility. For posts where the central question is what *ought* to be done.

**`politics`** - Policy, social justice, political commentary, civic life. Broader than `ethics`; includes posts about elections, legislation, and social movements.

**`career`** - Hiring, management, professional development, workplace culture, salary equity. For posts about navigating professional life in tech.

**`book-reviews`** - Reviews of specific books, whether technical or otherwise. More precise than the current `reading` label, which was also used for reading lists and recommendations.

**`technical-writing`** - Writing documentation, tutorials, papers, and books about technical topics. For posts about the craft of explaining technical ideas in prose.

**`fiction`** - Creative writing: stories, novels-in-progress, poetry. For posts that are themselves fiction or primarily about writing fiction.

**`personal`** - Personal reflections, autobiographical writing, essays about the author's own life and experience. Includes family matters.

**`humor`** - Satirical, comedic, or playful posts where the primary register is funny rather than analytical.

**`retro`** - Retrospectives, historical perspectives, anniversary reflections, posts that look back at how things were.

**`noticed`** - Reviews of, or reflections on, other people's posts, or film, music, visual arts, and other non-book media.

**`sdgc`** - keep existing posts labeled this way.

**`favorite`** - keep existing labels in place, but potentially add others to these posts.

**`anecdote`** - keep existing labels in place, but potentially add others to these posts.
